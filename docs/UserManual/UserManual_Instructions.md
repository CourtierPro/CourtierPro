## Instructions
**Date Due:** Friday, February 27, 2026

Once your system is deployed, your client, their employees, and other stakeholders will need to use the system. This document will explain to them, in clear steps, how to use the system, that means, how to accomplish all the use cases your system implements.

---

### Aspect 1 (Technical Quality) - 50%
The document is prepared as a high-quality text in terms of technical content. Following are some main criteria to address:
* The document is written in a user-facing manner.
* All implemented use cases are included in the document in a clear and easy to find manner.
* There are no errors or ambiguities in the use case instructions.
* The contents of the document are logically organized and use cases for specific actors can be located with a click from the table of contents.
* All the references to sections and figures are hyperlinked.
* Use case steps are clear and easy to follow.
* The steps are supported by images/screenshots when appropriate.
* The images are intertwined with the steps.
* Relevant elements in any included images are clearly identified.

### Aspect 2 (Document Organization and Formatting) - 20%
The document is prepared as a high-quality text in terms of academic writing standards. Following are some main criteria to address:
* The document includes cover page, names of the authors, revision history, table of contents, table of figures, list of tables, and contributions of the authors (See Aspect 3).
* All the diagrams are formatted in a professional manner.
* Charts, diagrams, and tables fit the page.
* The document uses consistent and appropriate fonts, font sizes, and styles.
* Links are clickable and lead to the correct place.
* The sections are numbered appropriately, and you have added explanatory text where needed, so the reader can understand the document. That means, each section must start with a short introduction, and then the diagrams and figures are shown.
* Figures are numbered and included in the list of figures.
* Tables are numbered and included in the list of tables.
* Any references to other documents are cited in a "References" section at the end of the document.

### Aspect 4 (English Literacy) -20%
Produce a document that meets academic writing standards, that means it:
* Uses precise and unambiguous language appropriate for technical content
* Is free of spelling, grammar, and punctuation errors
